TACET

TACET is a tool intended to aid or supplement flight simulator training. It is meant to address the issue of having to travel to a specific location to access a simulator by offering a training option that can be used from home. The tool is geared towards experienced pilots with a working knowledge of airplane flying. TACET features a meticulously rendered Airbus A320 model that provides an immersive training experience in a realistic spatial environment. While the training does not focus on actual flying, pilots are assigned tasks and their performance is recorded for later review during a debrief session. During the debrief, the pilots can watch their recorded 3D performance and discuss it with a trainer.

In my role as a game designer for this project, I was responsible for various tasks. These included creating documentation, such as the game design document, and collaborating with pilots to ensure that the cockpit was accurately presented in terms of avionic readouts and illuminated buttons. I also worked closely with pilots to understand the tasks and flows that they undertake, and then ensured that these were accurately represented in the gamified tasks. Additionally, I worked in close collaboration with 3D artists to achieve the final design of the cockpit, even going so far as to attend the task of scanning the cockpit with a lidar scanner. Furthermore, I was tasked with designing both the main menu and the competency menu, working alongside the artists to ensure that the desired feel was effectively conveyed. Finally, I created the tutorial that the pilots would use at the outset of their training, which included demonstrating how to interact with the buttons and make use of the competency menu.
