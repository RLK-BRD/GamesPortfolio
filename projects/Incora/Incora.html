INCORA

INCORA is software for training Infection, Contamination and Radiation protection skills. It contains simulation functionality that we have developed and field-tested with partners such as Innovate UK, Sellafield Ltd and the NHS. The primary focus of this software is to effectively demonstrate the inherent dangers and potential risks associated with contamination, highlighting how effortlessly it can spread. The Radiation Protection Training is designed as a collaborative activity, wherein a single participant utilizes the VR kit. Meanwhile, their teammates must assist them by providing information from their handbook to navigate through the software. Communication plays a crucial role, as the non-VR users are limited to observing static cameras that may not capture the entirety of the scene. The objective is for the team to successfully complete this exercise with minimal radiation exposure and minimal risk of contamination.

As game designer on this project, I initially began by familiarising myself with the concept of how Radiation travels and the principles of Inverse Square Law (the intensity of which the radiation goes down by the square by the distance of the source). Once I understood the dangers of how easily spread contamination can be, we had to think how to gamify this concept. Knowing that this application will be used not only to train for the concept of radiation, contamination and team building. It had to be inclusive for multiple users with limited headsets. Therefore it was decided that one member of the team/party would don the VR and the others would perform a supporting role. Once the concept had been established I had to design the area with three activities the participant must complete. In the scene were two sources of radiation, which if the participant is being observant can be detected by scanning the area with a dosimeter. In the higher dosage areas the participant is able to create a barrier using shielding provided in the scene. This can heavily reduce the amount of radiation that is absorbed. I placed the tasks in positions which would give a high dosage reading, and the more difficult tasks to complete were located in the areas which have the highest dosage. The final product offers a challenge which encourages teamwork and fosters preparation and pre planning. Most likely if the participant were to try and get through the task(s) as quickly as possible, they would receive a high dose rate and a poor final score. It was important that the core learning objectives were emphasised and highlighted in a fun and accessible way, allowing anyone with limited experience with VR the ability to train, learn, and enjoy the tool.
