Throw Development

Dodgebrawl focused on a simple gameplay loop which is find ball, pick up ball, throw ball. The video below documents the development of the process at different stages of the game. Throwing can be charged by holding the right trigger, the longer the throw is charged, the faster it will be released. Depending on the stats of the player, the released throw will have a faster or slower base. The charged throw is multiplied by the base level, so each characters fully charged throw is still respective to the stats set. When the ball is red, it indicates that it can deal damage. After two bounces and the speed drops to '0.5' the state can then change to 'pickupable' which becomes identifiable by turning yellow. My role included scripting and implementing the mechanics, and playtesting them closely with the team.

Another mechanic I was in charge of implementing is catching. When the ball is red, just before it makes contact with the player, the player can pull the left trigger. On a successful catch the player is rewarded a temporary period where their throw is fully charged and avoids losing a life and being knocked down. The catch sphere size was different for each character and was important when applying stats for different playstyles.
